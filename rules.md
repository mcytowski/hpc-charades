# HPC Charades Rules
Charades can be played with many different rule variations. The instructions below describe just one possible way to play the game.

The game is played by two teams. There is no specific limit on number of players in the team, but there should be equal number of players in each team.
- The game is divided into rounds, which proceed as follows:
  - A player from Team A draws a slip containing an HPC phrase.
  - After reviewing the phrase, the game moderator starts the timer and signals the player to begin acting.
  - Team A has three minutes to guess the phrase.
    - If the team correctly guesses the phrase within the time limit, one point is awarded.
    - If the phrase is not guessed within three minutes, time is called and no point is awarded.
  - A player from Team B then draws a phrase slip and follows the same process.
- The game typically continues until each player has had at least one turn acting out a phrase.
- At the end of the game, the team with the highest score wins.

### Acting Guidelines
- When acting out a phrase, players usually begin by indicating the number of words in the phrase.
- Words are then acted out one at a time, though not necessarily in order.
